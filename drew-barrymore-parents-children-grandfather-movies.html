Drew Barrymore’s grandfather

Drew Barrymore’s grandfather, John Barrymore, was part of a famous acting family that started performing on stage together in the early 1900s.

John was known for his remarkable performances in Shakespeare’s plays, especially his role as Hamlet, which made him famous as one of the best tragic actors in America at the time.

Later, he became a silent film actor, starred in popular movies like Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde, and played Sherlock Holmes in a 1922 movie.

This earned him the nickname “The Great Profile.” John, like his family, worked in both silent and sound movies.

As time went on, John Barrymore became even more famous when he transitioned to sound films.

Three of his most famous movies, Grand Hotel, Twentieth Century, and Midnight, are considered important in American film history and are kept in the National Film Registry.

Unfortunately, John had some personal problems, including issues with alcohol, which affected his later years.

This made movie studios hesitant to hire him, and he only appeared in a few more films before he passed away in 1942.

Nevertheless, John Barrymore is remembered and celebrated for his significant contributions to the film industry.

There are programs, awards, and even a Hollywood Star dedicated to him in his honor.
