Authorities Recover Body of Missing Georgia Duck Hunter in Alabama

The body of 22-year-old Brooks Edmund Hardie, a missing duck hunter from Georgia, was recovered early Monday morning from Lake Guntersville in Alabama where his kayak had capsized over the weekend.

Hardie was an experienced outdoorsman and athlete who tragically drowned after his kayak overturned during a solo hunting trip on Saturday morning. Authorities had been searching the area nonstop since his disappearance.

Promising Young Life Cut Short

A native of Bremen, Georgia, Hardie was a 2021 graduate of Bremen High School where he starred on the football and baseball teams. The tragic duck hunting accident took the life of a beloved son, classmate and teammate who was just 22 years old.

Hardie’s high school baseball coach, Davis Russel, emotionally eulogized the loss of someone who bettered the lives of all who knew him, writing: “The world is a better place because you were in it.”

Solo Duck Hunting Trip Ends in Tragedy

According to authorities, Hardie launched his kayak around 8 AM Saturday morning into Lake Guntersville near Goose Pond Resort. When his vessel overturned, he was pulled beneath the water with no chance to recover. Search crews immediately began combing the lake but hopes dimmed as hours passed with no sign.

His body was finally located using sonar technology and recovered early Monday, bringing some tragic closure to the accident almost two full days later. Investigators say Hardie was wearing a life jacket but frigid winter waters complicated rescue efforts.

Outpouring of Grief Over Lost Sports Star

As word spread of the drowning, Hardie’s tight-knit Georgia community reacted with profound grief over the loss of a popular and gifted student-athlete. Bremen High School announced counseling services while praising Hardie’s positive influence.

Bremen City Schools shared in a statement: “Please pray for Brooks’ family and friends during this very difficult time.” On social media, heartfelt tributes and condolences continue pouring in from classmates, teammates, friends and coaches.

Lasting Legacy

At just 22 years old, Brooks Edmund Hardie leaves behind grieving loved ones including parents Jason and Amy and sister Ashton. A Fundly campaign has been launched to help the family lay Brooks to rest. Though his accidental passing fills many hearts with immense sadness, his enduring brightness and passion for life continue shining.
