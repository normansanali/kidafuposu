Ilze Melngailis Age

Ilze Melngailis Job

Ilze Melngailis started her career as a Research Manager at Harvard Medical School & Boston Children’s Hospital in September 1990 and May 1992. From 1993 to 1994, she worked for Latvia’s Association for Family Planning and Sexual Health as Founder, Executive Director in Riga, Latvia.

In 1996, Ilze joined International Planned Parenthood Federation/Western Hemisphere Region as a Sustainability Analyst. She worked in that role until 1998. For two years (2001-2003), she also worked as a Consultant to International Women’s and Youth Programs at Independent Consultant.

She worked as a senior advisor of Marketing and Promotion for Meridian Group International from 2003 to 2006.

Furthermore, Ilze joined GBCHealth as Director of Corporate Strategies and Member Services. She then became Vice President, Partnership and Impact Initiatives.

Starting in 2012, Ilze has been serving as Senior Director of Global Partnerships and Senior Director of Business Council for the UN and Private Sector Engagement.

Ilze earned her degree in Psychology from McGill University in Canada in 1990. She earned her MBA in Marketing, Strategy from Yale School of Management.

Is Ilze Melngailis Married?

Yes, Ilze Melngailis is married to her husband Yucel Erdogan. According to The Knot, they registered their wedding on 18 October 2014 in Walpole, New Hampshire.

Her husband Yucel is a Turkish-born American artist based in New York. He has been living in the states for over 30 years. He is currently serving as the executive creative director of Avrett Free Ginsberg since 2015.

Yucel is also the founder/president/chief creative officer of Reason-Y. He had previously worked for Blue Flame as The Creative Director and art director at Lotas Minard Patton McIver from 1992 to 1999.

Yücel’s award-winning campaigns have aired around the world, including for brands such as Van Clef Arpels, Tumi, Natori, Nike, Bacardi, Dewar’s, and Ciroc. Also, an accomplished illustrator and photographer, the New York Times, Wall Street Journal, and Departures magazine has published his work.

He earned his MFA in Graphic Design from Marmara University and MSC in Communication Design from Pratt Institute.
