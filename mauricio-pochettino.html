Mauricio Pochettino is a former football player and professional football coach from Argentina. Mauricio Pochettino is the manager of the Premier League team “Tottenham Hotspur.” In the 2002 FIFA World Cup, he represented Argentina.

Early life

Mauricio Roberto Pochettino Trossero best known as Mauricio Pochettino was born on 2nd March 1972, in Murphy, Santa Fe, Argentina. As of now, he is 50 years old and his ethical background is unknown.

Moreover, he has a birth sign of Pisces and he is a citizen of Argentina. Talking about his parents, he is the son of Hector Pochettino (father) and Amalia Pochettino (mother).

In addition, he has two brothers whose names are unknown to the public. He was raised in a farming family that had worked the land for three generations. His ancestors are of Piedmontese origin.

Between the ages of eight and ten, he participated in football, volleyball, and judo. As a kid, he was a fan of Racing Club de Avellaneda.

Talking about his academic qualification, he attended a school that specialized in agriculture, Murphy. Moreover, there is no more information regarding his graduation.

Professional career

Mauricio Pochettino’s ability was noticed by José Griffa when he was 14 years old, and he began his football career with “Newell’s Old Boys” in 1987. He then made his First Division debut, which marked the start of his professional career as a central defender. \

Moreover, he subsequently transferred to RCD Espanyol, a Spanish football club, at the age of 22 in 1994, and quickly established himself as an established player in the team.

He stayed with the club for six and a half years, helping them win the Copa del Rey in 2000. In 2oo1, he was signed by Paris Saint-German Football Club (PSG). During his time, he was a regular member. As a result, he returned to Espanyol, where he spent more than two years.

International career

Talking about his international career, he debuted while playing for the U23 Argentine team at the CONMEBOL Men’s Olympic Qualifying Tournament in Paraguay in 1992.

In his four-year career, he played 20 times for his country. He also took part in the 2002 FIFA World Cup, where he performed on the field.

As a manager

He began his role as a manager in 2009 when he made his La Liga debut with FC Espanyol. In 2009, he was appointed as Espanyol’s third coach, a post he maintained until 2012.

Then it was revealed that he would be the next manager of the Southampton Premier League club’s first team in 2013. After Osvaldo Ardiles, he was the second Argentine coach in English football.

later, he became a head coach at the English Premier League Club Southampton in 2013. As a head coach, he received his first victory over the Manchester City Club which made the club secure the eighth position.

He was the tenth manager of “The Spurs,” and he led the team to the League Cup final. Later, he joined Tottenham Hotspur, where he earned the title of “Club Administrator” after giving up his contract with the club until 2021.
